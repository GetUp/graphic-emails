Putting an end to the ‘Advocacy Gag Law’

The Coalition’s original advocacy gag law was a full scale assault by the Hard Right on GetUp, the charity sector, and civil society at large. It would have choked off our people-powered fundraising, and forced us to give up our political independence.1

But GetUp members stepped up and fought back like never before. Together with our friends in civil society, especially the Hands Off Our Charities Alliance we left the Coalition with no choice but to amend the bill to within an inch of its life.

How did we do it? Together we launched the biggest campaign blitz in our history with over 40,000 phone calls, emails, letters-to-the-editor, Facebook posts, tweets, in-person visits, postal letters and even good old faxes.

We made sure the voices of everyday people were front and centre everywhere our politicians looked.

We’ve shown once again that when we all come together, our movement is more powerful than anything the Hard Right can throw at us.

Powering Our Civil Defence Fund

The Hard Right’s relentless attacks have meant GetUp needs the best legal firepower available to us. Thanks to the thousands of GetUp members who make contributions to our Civil Defence Fund, we’ve been able to weather the storm and concentrate on keeping our crucial campaigns on track.

The Hard Right launched their third attempt to have the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) force GetUp members to become an ‘associated entity’ of other political parties.2 The ongoing investigation has been exhausting, but thanks to everyone who chipped into our Civil Defence Fund, it hasn’t dragged precious resources away from the fight for the issues we care about.

The people who power our Civil Defence Fund also made it possible for us to obtain the best legal advice available to ascertain how the Coalition’s draconian new ‘national security’ laws would affect our campaigns.3 This ongoing advice has been crucial in allowing us to continue fearlessly campaigning for a fair, flourishing and just future.

Most recently, this people-powered funding paid for crucial legal advice that allowed us to shine a light on the medical emergency for children detained in Australia's cruel offshore detention regime on Nauru. Because of that, 107 kids like George* and their families have now been brought to safety in Australia.
